Types of Therapy

Psychodynamic Therapy
This type of therapy originates from psychoanalysis, which is a long-term approach to treatment for mental health. In psychoanalysis, you can talk about anything that has been on your mind to recognize hidden thought or behavior patterns that are potentially contributing to your distress. It's also common to discuss your childhood or past.
Behavioral Therapy
This type of therapy is concentrated on finding healthy coping strategies regarding mental health. Behavioral theory states that particular behaviors stem from things you learned in your past, resulting in distress or other negative impacts.
Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
CBT is a short-term approach for treating mental health issues. It's similar to behavioral therapy, how it also confronts intrusive thought patterns or troublesome thoughts.
Humanistic Therapy
This type of therapy is an approach that dissects how your worldview impacts your choices, especially those that cause you distress. It comes from "the belief that you're the best person to understand your experiences and needs" (Raypole).
